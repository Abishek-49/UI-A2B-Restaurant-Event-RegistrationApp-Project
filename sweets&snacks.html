Laddu : These laddus are a great source of antioxidants that will help to reduce the free radicals in the body. Meaning, having a laddu might lower the chances of you catching heart diseases and more. The antioxidants in the laddu help fight cell damage and lower the risk of chronic diseases.

Making
  1. We make laddu by slow roasting gram flour in ghee until it turns aromatic and golden in color.
  2. This nutty mixture is cooled down
  3. And then mixed with powdered cardamoms and sugar.
  4. Toppings with cashews, pistachios and melon seeds as well.

Samosa (veg) :  Vegetable samosas can provide a good source of vitamins and minerals, like vitamin A and vitamin C. Baked or fried in healthy oil, like olive oil, samosas can be a low-fat snack option. Some samosa fillings, like potatoes, are a good source of fiber, which is important for digestive health.

Making
  1. Prepared with an all-purpose flour (locally known as maida) and stuffed with a filling,
  2. Mixture of diced and cooked or mashed boiled potato,
  3. Added with onions, green peas,
  4. Followed by stuffing lentils, ginger, spices and green chili.
  5. Finally the stuffed veggies is folded in triangle shape and fried in fresh coconut oil served with tomato sauce as dip
